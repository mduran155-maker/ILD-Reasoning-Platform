## 1. Purpose

This document opens v0.7 — Non-UIP Fibrotic ILD Differential Reasoning Expansion.

It does not diagnose hypersensitivity pneumonitis.  
It does not diagnose CTD-ILD.  
It does not diagnose fibrotic NSIP.  
It does not diagnose sarcoidosis.  
It does not diagnose occupational ILD.  
It does not diagnose drug toxicity.  
It does not diagnose aspiration-related ILD.  
It does not recommend treatment.  
It does not decide biopsy.  
It does not create public readiness.  
It does not replace radiologist interpretation, pathology review, clinical correlation, microbiology, pulmonology, rheumatology, occupational medicine, MDD, or urgent clinical assessment.

Its purpose is to open a source-governed reasoning space for non-UIP fibrotic ILD differentials.

The controlling principle is:

> “Differential reasoning is not differential diagnosis authority.”

The inherited platform principle remains:

> “It teaches clinicians to read change before naming disease.”

For v0.7, this becomes:

> “Read differential pattern, source status, mimics, missing evidence, and review need before naming non-UIP fibrotic ILD.”

## 5. Source Role Boundaries

### 5.1 HP Guideline Role

The HP guideline may support future source mapping for:

- HP as a differential diagnosis in newly identified ILD,
- fibrotic vs nonfibrotic HP distinction,
- exposure history and inciting antigen reasoning,
- serum IgG as exposure-supporting evidence,
- BAL lymphocytosis reasoning,
- HRCT features such as mosaic attenuation, air trapping, three-density pattern, centrilobular nodules, and fibrotic HP overlap,
- HP MDD reasoning.

It must not be used to:

- diagnose HP automatically,
- replace exposure review,
- replace MDD,
- recommend treatment,
- decide biopsy automatically.

---

### 5.2 IIP Classification Role

The IIP classification source may support future mapping for:

- idiopathic NSIP as a distinct clinicopathologic entity,
- chronic fibrosing IIP categories,
- organizing pneumonia and fibrotic organizing pneumonia overlap,
- smoking-related IIP,
- acute/subacute IIP,
- rare IIP,
- unclassifiable IIP,
- coexisting patterns,
- disease behavior classification,
- dynamic multidisciplinary diagnosis.

It must not be used to:

- make platform diagnosis,
- collapse mixed patterns into a single label,
- automate biopsy decisions,
- create treatment authority.

---

### 5.3 SARD-ILD Guideline Role

The SARD-ILD guideline may support future mapping for:

- systemic autoimmune rheumatic disease ILD screening and monitoring context,
- RA, systemic sclerosis, idiopathic inflammatory myopathy, mixed connective tissue disease, and Sjögren-related ILD risk context,
- HRCT and PFT screening/monitoring reasoning,
- shared decision-making,
- guideline uncertainty and patient-specific clinical application.

It must not be used to:

- diagnose CTD-ILD automatically,
- replace rheumatology/pulmonology evaluation,
- recommend treatment,
- create screening orders,
- become a public-facing triage tool.

---

### 5.4 Sarcoidosis Guideline Role

The sarcoidosis guideline may support future mapping for:

- sarcoidosis as a granulomatous differential,
- compatible clinical presentation,
- nonnecrotizing granulomatous inflammation,
- exclusion of alternative granulomatous causes,
- sarcoidosis diagnostic uncertainty,
- lymphadenopathy / perilymphatic / extrapulmonary clue reasoning,
- close follow-up and diagnostic caution.

It must not be used to:

- diagnose sarcoidosis automatically,
- ignore infection, malignancy, berylliosis, HP, drug reaction, or other granulomatous mimics,
- replace tissue/pathology review where needed,
- recommend treatment.

## 9. Core v0.7 Reasoning Principles

### 9.1 Differential Reasoning Is Not Diagnosis

The platform may say:

- “HP-like differential prompt is visible.”
- “CTD-ILD context is missing.”
- “NSIP-like pattern should remain visible.”
- “Sarcoid-like granulomatous differential remains visible.”
- “Occupational exposure review is incomplete.”

The platform must not say:

- “HP diagnosed.”
- “CTD-ILD confirmed.”
- “NSIP diagnosed.”
- “Sarcoidosis diagnosed.”
- “Occupational ILD diagnosed.”

---

### 9.2 Pattern Is Not Disease

A CT pattern, pathology pattern, or clinical clue may support a reasoning prompt.

It does not create final disease diagnosis.

Examples:

- mosaic attenuation does not diagnose HP,
- NSIP-like imaging does not diagnose idiopathic NSIP,
- perilymphatic nodules do not diagnose sarcoidosis,
- pleural plaques do not diagnose asbestosis by platform,
- organizing pneumonia pattern does not diagnose cryptogenic organizing pneumonia by platform.

---

### 9.3 Missing Evidence Is Not Negative Evidence

If exposure history is missing, HP is not excluded.

If autoimmune context is missing, CTD-ILD is not excluded.

If medication history is missing, drug toxicity is not excluded.

If infection data are missing, infection is not excluded.

If occupational history is missing, occupational ILD is not excluded.

If pathology context is missing, diagnosis is not settled.

If MDD is missing, integrated diagnosis is not established.

---

### 9.4 Coexisting Processes Are Allowed

v0.7 must inherit v0.3 superimposed-events logic.

The platform must allow coexistence of:

- UIP-facing fibrosis plus HP-like small airway features,
- CTD-ILD plus OP-like pattern,
- sarcoidosis plus infection mimic,
- smoking-related emphysema plus fibrosis,
- fibrotic ILD plus acute infection,
- fibrotic ILD plus edema,
- fibrotic ILD plus hemorrhage,
- chronic ILD plus acute exacerbation-like overlay.

Single-label closure remains blocked.

---

### 9.5 MDD Is the Integration Surface

v0.7 must treat MDD as the review surface for:

- discordant clinical, radiologic, and pathologic evidence,
- mixed pattern disease,
- uncertain exposure history,
- possible CTD-ILD,
- fibrotic HP vs IPF/NSIP overlap,
- sarcoid-like granulomatous differential,
- unclassifiable ILD,
- pathology/imaging mismatch,
- working diagnosis review.

MDD prompt is not a diagnosis.

MDD prompt is review routing.
